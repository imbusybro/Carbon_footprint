Energy Transition Insights
Global renewable share is slowly rising — From ~6% in 1965 to ~13.5% today. Hydropower dominates, but wind/solar are growing fastest.
Iceland and Norway lead — Thanks to abundant hydro and geothermal resources, they achieve >70% renewable share.
Large emitters lag behind — China, India, and the USA have renewable shares below 20%, though absolute renewable capacity is growing rapidly.
Fossil fuels still dominate — Even in leading countries, fossil fuels often provide the majority of total energy consumption.
