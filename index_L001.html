Key Insights
Global emissions continue to rise — CO₂ output has increased over 240% since 1965, with a slight plateau post‑2010 but no sustained decline.
China, USA, and India dominate — Together they account for nearly half of global emissions. China surpassed the USA around 2006.
Emission intensity is improving — Most countries show decreasing tonnes per $M GDP over decades, indicating economic decoupling.
Per‑capita disparities remain huge — Qatar, UAE, and Kuwait emit over 30 tonnes/person, while India and most African nations are below 2 tonnes.
