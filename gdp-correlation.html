GDP–CO₂ Correlation Insights
Strong positive correlation — Larger economies almost always emit more CO₂, but the slope (intensity) varies dramatically.
High‑intensity outliers — Trinidad & Tobago, Turkmenistan, and Venezuela show >15 tonnes per $M GDP due to heavy fossil fuel extraction.
Decoupling is visible — Many European nations (France, Sweden) have reduced emissions while growing GDP, thanks to nuclear and renewables.
Asia dominates total emissions — Driven by population and manufacturing, Asia accounts for over 50% of global CO₂.
